Timeline

  • 1941: January 5: Born in the town of Akebono-cho in Bunkyō, Tokyo
  • 1944: He and his family were evacuated to Utsonomiya
  • 1945: Evacuated to Kanuma after the bombing of Utsunomiya in the same year
  • 1963: Employed at Toei Animation as an in-between artist on various theatrical and televised anime, and later as chief animator, concept artist, and scene designer on The Great Adventure of Horus, Prince of the Sun (1968).
  • 1969 - 1970: Under the pseudonym Akitsu Saburō (秋津 三朗), Miyazaki wrote and illustrated the manga People of the Desert
  • 1971: Miyazaki leaves Toei Animations and is hired at A-pro as director/codirector
  • 1973: Miyazaki moves from A-Pro to Zuiyō Eizō, which later becomes Nippon Animation in 1975.
  • 1979: Miyazaki leaves Nippon Animation, moving to Telecom Animation Film (a TMS Entertainnment subsidiary).
